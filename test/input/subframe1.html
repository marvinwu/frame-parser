Leaders of the Independence movement

\n

Gopal Krishna Gokhale 1866 – 1915 Gokhale was an early leader of the Indian\n National Congress. Gokhale supported social and political reform which would give India greater autonomy. He was\n considered a moderate – working with British institutions and opposing more direct approaches to independence.\n Gokhale was an important mentor to Gandhi.

\n
\n

Mahatma Gandhi (1869 – 1948) The\n foremost political leader of the Indian independence movement. For over two decades, Gandhi led a peaceful\n independence movement, characterised by non-violent protests, such as boycotts and the Salt March. He commanded\n respect from both Hindus and Muslim, but, despite seeking a united India, was unable to avoid the partition of 1947.\n He was assassinated by a Hindu fanatic in 1948.

\n
\n

Sri Aurobindo (1872 – 1950) One of the key figures in the early\n Indian Independence movement, Aurobindo initiated early efforts at full independence and was sympathetic to armed\n resistance. After his retreat to a spiritual ashram, he rarely spoke on political matters apart from in 1942, where\n he urged Congress and Gandhi to accept the Cripps proposal to give Indian Dominion status. Sri Aurobindo became a\n noted philosopher, poet and Spiritual Teacher.

\n
\n

Ali Jinnah (1876 – 1948) Jinnah was leader of the All-India Muslim league\n from 1913 to 1947 and then as Pakistan’s first Governor. Initially, Jinnah advocated Hindu-Muslim unity and\n supported the All-India Home Rule League. But from 1940, he rejected the idea of a united India and advocated an\n independent Muslim state of Pakistan.

\n
\n

Sir Muhammad Iqbal (1877 – 21 April 1938) Iqbal was an Islamic\n poet, philosopher and politician. As President of the All-Muslim League, Iqbal was influential in promoting the idea\n of separate Muslim provinces and ultimately was influential in encouraging Jinnah to embrace the idea of a separate\n nation of Pakistan

\n
\n

Jawaharlal Nehru \n (1889-1964) – Nehru was an influential nationalist from the 1910s. With the backing of Gandhi, he came to lead\n Congress, moving the party to the left and seeking a united independent India. After Congress was politically\n diminished after the British crackdown on the ‘Quit India’ movement of 1942, Congress was unable to\n prevent the partition of India. Nehru became the first Prime Minister of India.

\n
\n

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose (1897-1945) Indian\n nationalist leader. Netaji raised a united Indian army (INA) of all religious faiths in an attempt to gain\n independence for India through military means.
